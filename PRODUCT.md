# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Gym owners, head trainers, and solo coaches at different scales use Scout during real coaching work: onboarding clients, running questionnaires, building and adjusting programs, reviewing progress (including body-composition scans), and managing trainers and exercise libraries. Context is busy floor or desk time between sessions; they need speed without feeling replaced by generic AI output.

## Product Purpose

Scout is an AI training companion for coaches who care about personalized programming. It helps gyms and coaching teams create and refine client-specific training plans faster while preserving the coach's judgment, voice, and methodology. Success looks like a coach shipping a thoughtful, individualized program in less time than manual spreadsheet work, with confidence that Scout accelerated the work rather than homogenizing it.

## Brand Personality

**Energetic · Direct · Warm**

Voice is supportive and clear, not corporate or cutesy. Scout speaks like a capable colleague on the gym floor: encouraging, specific, and action-oriented ("Build program", "Save changes") rather than jargon or empty hype. The companion brand adds warmth and loyalty without childish mascot overload.

## Anti-references

- Generic B2B SaaS: navy-and-purple gradients, hero-metric dashboards, identical icon-card grids, sterile "No data" empty states, buzzword copy (streamline, empower, seamless).
- Patterns that read as obvious AI UI: gradient text, glassmorphism-by-default, uppercase eyebrow kickers on every section, numbered section scaffolding without real sequence.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed with soul** — Optimize for faster program delivery; never trade away the coach's intent for template filler.
2. **The tool disappears** — UI should feel familiar to coaches who use serious productivity tools; affordances stay consistent across screens.
3. **Show the work** — Progress, job steps, and AI reasoning stay visible during long operations; coaches can leave and return without losing context.
4. **Warm professionalism** — B2B seriousness with human tone: direct labels, helpful empty states, errors that suggest the next step.
5. **Coach in control** — AI suggests; the coach edits, approves, and owns outcomes. Language and flows reinforce judgment, not automation theater.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text contrast, focus rings (signal), keyboard navigation, meaningful link and control labels.
- Respect **`prefers-reduced-motion`**: meaningful motion on reveals and transitions, with reduced or instant alternatives.
- Avoid relying on color alone for state; pair semantic colors with text, icons, or labels.
